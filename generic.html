My Story

I dreamt of this opportunity the night before. I felt nervous, even shaking. Not a minute passed since I last glanced at the clock. I paced around my room. Then ding, the alarm on my phone startled me out of deep thought. I pressed join and sat patiently waiting in front of my computer screen while the guests logged in. Soon, the supervisor began speaking. He gave a brief background about his path from being inside of classrooms to becoming founder of an educational organization. My anxiousness seemed to vanish until we were prompted to introduce ourselves. I opened my mouth, but all the air seemed to get stuck in my throat. Eventually, after calming myself down I spoke and everyone else did as well. Then, we received our first big project. My heart sank because I never had encountered a project such as this before. I felt comfortable with handheld physical projects, but virtual projects seemed out of my league. Overwhelmed with anxiety at the time, but I reassured myself that this was just another opportunity to learn. Right away I listened attentively to my supervisor ‘s instructions for as many instructions as I possibly could. Luckily, it was a group project, and I could collaborate with a peer. Our task involved creating an online course geared towards helping students in high school. First, I began by filling in my knowledge gaps. I went on YouTube and researched as much as I could about building an online course. I became familiar with WordPress and its wonderful features. I even found a front-end plug-in to bring an aesthetic design to the website page. I grew to love the website-building process and I knew it would pay off.

After I contacted my partner, we introduced ourselves and our goals as a team. Next, we started brainstorming the foundational content for the online course. We didn’t want our course to be just another disposable site with psychobabble. So, we decided to view it from a student’s perspective. We asked each other what we wished we would have known as high school students. We thought of different topics according to each grade level. For freshmen and sophomores, we prioritized time management and extracurricular participation. For juniors and seniors, we concurred with test taking skill assessment and resources for the college transition process.

With our course mapped out, we needed to implement our strategy. We divided the work among ourselves and completed different parts of the course. As I worked on organizing the website and its pages, I grew nervous. I became afraid to fail and potentially ruin the entire project. With careful progression, my fear subsided. During the entire project I learned by trial and error. For example, when I input data from the back end, the text either did not display correctly or appeared dull in the user interface. Therefore, I had to research and reattempt the same task until it came out perfectly.

Our deadline was approaching quickly, and my group had not finished. Concerned, I made a personal commitment to work overtime to complete our project. Sometimes, the website would take a long time to load and render objects. This was catastrophic. I had to constantly go back and forth between the front-end and back-end to ensure optimal viewer experience.

Finally, the course was completed a few days before the project deadline. My partner and I conducted quality assurance and fixed minor errors. My supervisor with our results. I believe that preparation and hard work helped me succeed. I felt accomplished after stepping out of my comfort zone. Less than three years later, I transitioned into the Honors College of Miami Dade College to major in computer science to ultimately pursue a career in website development.
